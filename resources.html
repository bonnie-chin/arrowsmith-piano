Jane encourages her students to explore their interests!

only be found in professional institutions, requiring extreme
dedication and monetary investment in order to
learn a more advanced, or unique skill.

Online courses and specializations are extremely
flexible and shorter-term opportunities to obtain
knowledge or even certification for exploring and cultivating
experience with certain fields from a variety of skill levels.

Some of the best organizations include Coursera,
Udacity and Udemy. From Music Biology to Audio Editing,
online courses for the music industry are diverse and endless.

MUSIC CAREERS

While traditional musical careers are still in place today, from classical performers to composers, music has evolved tremendously from its conventions.

For those seeking more modern, technological path, music technology and audio engineering are up and coming industries in the music realm. Music studies in relation to cognitive science and psychology are also rising areas of research.

  • A&R Coordinator
  • Music Therapist/Psychologist
  • Record Producer/Engineer
  • Arranger/Orchestrator
  • Mix Engineer



    • POST-SECONDARY EDUCATION

    Post-secondary education music programs can be found
    in almost every university in the world. However, each
    individual university offers different programs, emphasizing
    different aspects and portions of music as a whole.

    Julliard is an undeniably prestigious arts institution,
    however it focuses immensely more upon traditional
    forms of music and performance, which is excellent for
    those pursuing such a field.

    For others, many post secondary institutions such as
    Berklee and NYU have programs on some of the newest music
    industries worldwide, from modern music production and
    music therapy programs.


    The Schulich School of Music of McGill University is located in downtown Montreal, one of Canada's most vibrant and international cities.

    It is home to a diverse community of performers, composers, music theorists, musicologists, educators, scientists and engineers.


    The undergraduate programs at Berklee College of Music include a rigorous core curriculum, instruction on a range of principal instruments, and a wide variety of majors and minors. From internships nationwide to exchange programs in Spain, Berklee offers a diverse musical experience.



    The University of Toronto’s faculty of music offers a wide variety of musical majors, from combinations of Music and Health Science, Musicology to traditional performance degrees. U of T Music alumni make up almost a quarter of this year's classical top 30 under 30' list.



    The music programs at Carleton University provide a solid grounding in the practice, analysis and history of European classical music.

    They focus on more recently developed areas such as computer music, composition, jazz, ethnomusicology, popular music, and the sociology of music.
